Staff

  • About us


    Our goal is to improve the quality of life of our patients based on the bio-psycho-social model.
    From the beginning, we have followed the philosophy that our patients come first. No matter why or when you visit us, we provide comprehensive and excellent care.
    In 2005, we supervised the first general practice residency. In the meantime, there have been 14 colleagues who have accompanied us for between six and 24 months.
    Since 2007, we have been giving lectures and seminars at the University of Bonn in the training of medical students. These often come to our practice for internships and clinical traineeships.
    Also students of the practical year (PJ) are trained here at the end of their studies in the so-called elective subject of general medicine.

  • Career at BdB


    We have been training since August 2003. In the meantime, 10 MFAs (medical assistants), 16 doctors and 18 students in their practical year have successfully completed their training with us.

  • Medical Assistant (MFA)


    In relation to MFA training, it should be mentioned that our employee Ms. Petra Fiest has taken over the leadership in this regard due to manifold advanced training in a wide variety of fields. In addition, she also works as an examiner for MFA trainees at the Medical Association. The entire spectrum of activities of a general medical practice is taught. This includes, but is not limited to, communication (in person and by phone), registration, practical skills (drawing blood, performing EKGs, long-term blood pressure measurements and pulmonary function tests, on-site testing of blood and urine), handling inquiries, preparing prescriptions/referrals/referrals/etc.,
    manifold communication with health care providers (e.g., specialty practices, hospitals, laboratory medicine, physical therapy, nursing services, retirement homes).

  • Doctors


    If we already work in the most interesting profession in the world, we would like to practice this as well and with as much joy as possible. A good working atmosphere, in which everyone participates every day, makes it (mostly) possible.

  • Students


    As part of our activities as a teaching practice and lecturer, students in the practical year in the elective subject of general medicine are primarily trained with us. Unfortunately, we can only very seldom make appointments for clinical clerkships and block internships, as we do not want to supervise more than one student at a time. We attach great importance to the optimization of anamnestic arts (including the communication thereby) and physical examination methods. Further diagnostics (laboratory, ECG, LuFu, LZ-RR, sono abdomen and thyroid) can be performed generously if indicated, therapeutic measures should primarily include counseling on healthy lifestyle. We try to minimize medication use as much as possible through specific counseling.
